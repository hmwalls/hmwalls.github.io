About Me

I grew up in Southern California where I learned how to apply sunscreen constantly, developed a deep appreciation for mexican food, and practiced what to do in case of an earthquake. I participated in that time-honored tradition of fleeing your home town after high school and made my way to Portland, Oregon.

After arriving in the great nation of Cascadia, I spent a few years at Lewis & Clark College, where I got my degree in Biology. I bought proper rain gear, developed a taste for microbrews, and learned how to enjoy camping.

Now, I spend half of my week at the Oregon Zoo, where I work in youth programs. My job duties include affixing googly eyes to things, managing and analyzing program data, creating fun and interesting presentations, and shopping for fake scat (no, really).

The other half of my week belongs to consulting. Often this means websites or other computer-centric work, but I'm open to all sorts of fun projects. Birding trips? I know all the best spots. Wine parties? I can help you plan one that will go down in history. Copy writing and editing? I'm your girl (even if you need limericks [and especially if you need a haiku]).

In the internet age and an industry where trust is key, having a great web presence was critical for this massage therapist. I worked with the client to create a minimalist design that highlighted gorgeous photos of his studio and services.

Photography Website

Custom responsive small business website using Bootstrap.

This photographers's stunning portrait and event photographs were languishing in Facebook albumns and folders on his computer. I created a simple, single page site that showcased the photos. The site is responsive, so his images look beautiful on all screen sizes.

Survey Results Infographic

Paper surveys and messy Excel spreadsheets turned into an attractive, fun infographic.

The Youth Mentoring Collective held a job fair for diverse youth and collected a pile of paper surveys full of valuable information from participants. I helped to organize, clean up, and analyze the data. Instead of handing over the results as a boring Excel file, I created a colorful, easy to read infographic and matching presentation. Event organizers gained valuable insight and can refer to the infographic as they design their next job fair, as well as share it as part of future fundraising efforts.

Star Trek Safety Training

A notoriously dull training presentation made fun and memorable.

Each year at the Oregon Zoo over 100 new teenage volunteers arrive, ready to work all summer. Before they head out, each teen goes through close to 25 hours of training. One key piece of the training is safety protocol and radio use. In years past, the presentation was bare bones and pretty dull. I recreated the training with fun, themed visuals and a conversational style. The training has been requested by several other departments and the teens feel much more prepared than they previously have.

Birdathon 2015

A 12 hour birding trip from Portland to Tillamook and back, with 123 species seen.

Birdathon is a fundraising birding trip for Audubon Society of Portland. I co-lead the Raven Maniacs team in 2015. Our team went from Portland to Tillamook and back, stopping a many prime birding locations along the way. Over the course of the day, we managed to find 123 species of birds - a new team record. Highlights included Tufted Puffins at Haystack rock, an American Pipit singing at Fernhill Wetlands, and ending the day with a American Bitter, Sora, and Virginia Rail trifecta at Killin Wetlands.
